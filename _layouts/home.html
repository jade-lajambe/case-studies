Overview

Remitly is the largest digital money transfer service in the United States; transforming the lives of immigrants and their families by empowering their users.

After raising $115M of funding from PayU, they needed to prepare for rapid product and team expansion. The signed-out and signed-in website was comprised of static modules and features relying on the Design and Engineering teams for every change.

These teams needed a source of truth. PM and Marketing needed a CMS that would make them autonomous and prepared for expansion.

My role was to create the UI guidelines, design the signed-in and signed-out site pages, and work with the dev team to identify the components and functions the CMS would need.

Define the challenge

Product: Remitly was scaling from 9 send-receive corridors to over 300,which made their current ad hoc website, product and digital content untenable.

Company: The Remitly team was opening offices in two new locations and doubling the size of their team from 350 to 700+. Rapid expansion was leading to teams recreating the same assets and causing an inconsistent user experience.

We designed a sustainable CMS that aligned the teams and allowed us to hit our expansion goals.

Discovery

  • RACI model to identify stakeholders
  • Site inventory to define UI patterns and component types needed for each phase of launch
  • User testing to surface any major UX concerns
  • Identifying dynamic content to minimize effort and prevent unnecessary human error

Discovery: User Testing

Scaling and consistency were the main goals. To meet the expansion timeline this wasn’t the time to redo the architecture or content, however, I ran some user tests to make sure I was aware of any major UX errors that could be corrected during this redesign. Three show-stoppers were identified:

Users were unable to identify and navigate corridors: Desktop (Validately), Mobile (Validately).

0/30 users were able to correctly state what Remitly’s exchange rates were on the pricing page: Pricing (Validately).

Users didn’t understand our product. Remitly’s Customer Service Team was receiving over 9,500 calls/mo for KYC inquiries, 6,400 calls/mo for tier limits, and 30% of all calls are to answer common questions that were not available on the site.

Ideate

Dynamic Content

Working with the Development team to identify dynamic text and image replacements, to establish consistency, minimize effort, and prevent unnecessary human error.

  • Customer name
  • Referer name
  • Send country name
  • Receive country name
  • Promotional rate
  • Current send rate
  • Current recieve rate
  • Fee
  • Flags
  • Icons
  • Logos
  • Background images
  • Color themes
  • Header
  • Footer
  • Disclaimers

Ideate: Component Types

I created wireframes of the top viewed pages to identify a lean set of components types needed for launch. Every component was redesigned and aligned. 5 new component types were created outside of the initial project scope to address the findings in the user testing we couldn’t ignore.

Design: UI Guidelines

Finally building for scale. Creating a shared set of guidelines eliminated duplicity, confusion and reduced design and development time. maintaining a symbol library.

Design: Final Components

We launched with 10 component types and now have 40. When creating a new component we ask ourselves if it needs to be a stand alone, or if better functionality can be added to something already existing.

Test

User testing: Validately, Fullstory, customer surveys, and CS Calls used to direct ongoing improvements to the user experience.

Internal: Training sessions with the PMs and Marketers to teach them how to use their new tool and get their feedback on improvements. Created a slack channel and bi-weekly office hours to discuss updates.

Conversion: Each page type underwent A/B testing to make sure that conversion wasn’t impacted negatively.

Results

Scale: Positioned the new CMS to be scalable and self-serviced to support the expansion to 300+ corridor combinations. Previously it took 5 days to launch a page after content was created, now several pages can be launched in minutes.

Autonomous: Marketers and PMs can now create tests and launch entire pages without needing the Design or Development teams.

Efficiency: Creating guidelines and a CMS decreased design and development time and duplicity.

Consistency: Guidelines created consistency internal and with external vendors across product and marketing materials.

UX: Users are able to change corridors, read pricing pages, find common questions, and navigate the site.

Conversion: Not the purpose of the project, but the launch of the CMS saw and 16% increase in conversion and the site saw a 30% reduction in bounce rate.

SEO: Also not the main scope, but site views improved by 20%. 85% of the pages/keywords being ranked top 5 and 95% appearing in first page results.
