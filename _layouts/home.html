Brief

Remitly is the largest digital money transfer service in the US. transforming the lives of immigrants and their families by empowering their users.

After raising $115M of funding from PayU they needed to prepare for rapid product and team expansion.

Define

Product: Remitly’s was scaling from 9 send receive corridors to over 300 which made their current static website, product and digital content unsustainable.

Company: The Remitly team was opening offices in two new locations and doubling the size of their headquarters. They needed an easy to use system that created an autonomous team, while ensuring consistency in the user lifecycle.

Solution: Create a CMS that could be used by PMs and Marketers to create the signed in and signed out site, newsletters, and digital marketing content.

Focus on site first to meet scaling needs.

Discovery

  • RACI model to identify stakeholders
  • Site inventory to define component types needed for launch and UI patterns
  • User testing to surface any major UX concerns
  • Identifying dynamic content to minimize effort and prevent unessisary human error

Discovery: User Testing

Users were unable to identify and navigate corridors: Desktop (Validately), Mobile (Validately).

0/30 users were able to correctly state what Remitly’s exchange rates were on the pricing page: Pricing (Validately).

Remitly’s Customer Service Team was recieving over 9,500 calls/mo for KYC inquieries, 6,400 calls/mo for tier limits, and 30% of all calls are to answer common questions that are not available on the site.

Ideate

Dynamic Content

Worked with the dev team to identified dynamic text and image replacements to establish consistency, minimize effort, and prevent unessisary human error.

  • Customer name
  • Referer name
  • Send country name
  • Receive country name
  • Promotional rate
  • Current send rate
  • Current recieve rate
  • Fee
  • Flags
  • Icons
  • Logos
  • Background images
  • Colour themes
  • Header
  • Footer
  • Disclaimers

Ideate: Component Types

Created wireframes of the top viewed pages to identify a lean set of components types needed for launch.

Test

User testing: Validately, Fullstory, customer surveys, and CS Calls used to direct ongoing improvements to the user expereince.

Internal: Training sessions with the PMs and Marketers to teach them how to use their new tool and get their feedback on improvements. Created a slack channel and bi-weekly office hours to discuss updates.

Conversion: Each page type underwent A/B testing to make sure that conversion wasn’t impacted negatively.

Results

Scale: Positioned the new CMS platform to be scalable and self-serviced to support an expansion to 300+ corridor combinations. Previously it took 5 days to launch a page after content was created, now pages can be launched in minutes.

Autonomous: Marketers and PMs can now create tests and launch entire pages without needing the Dev or Design team.

Efficiency: Creating an internal tool decreased design and development time and duplicity.

Consistency: Guidelines created consistency across product and marketing materials.

UX: Users are able to change corridors, read pricing pages and navigate the site.

Conversion: Increased 16% and the site saw a 30% reduction in bounce rate.

SEO: Improved site views by 20%. 85% of the pages/keywords being ranked top 5 and 95% appearing in first page results.
